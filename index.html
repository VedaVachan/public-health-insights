About U.S. Population

The United States has an estimated population of 347 million (34.7 crore), across 50 states and 5 inhabited territories — Puerto Rico, Guam, U.S. Virgin Islands, American Samoa, and the Northern Mariana Islands.

Healthcare Snapshot

Healthcare spending in the U.S. is nearly 18% of GDP. Public health agencies such as the CDC and NIH lead national efforts for disease prevention and surveillance.

Disease Awareness Facts

  • HIV: Over 1.2 million people in the U.S. live with HIV.
  • Diabetes: Affects nearly 11% of adults nationwide.
  • Tuberculosis: Though rare, remains a concern in high-density urban areas.
  • Hepatitis C: Leading cause of liver transplants in the U.S.
